T1-Dark Rim Lesion Training

This portal is intended to help imaging experts recognise the T1-dark rim lesion (T1-DRL), a novel sign of smouldering inflammation in multiple sclerosis ( Naval-Baudin et al., European Journal of Radiology, 2024 ).

5 guided batches 15 self-eval batches Up to 20 candidates per batch

Each batch contains up to 20 T1-DRL candidates that have been read multiple times by observers with different levels of experience. All candidates were initially tagged as possible T1-DRLs by at least one reader, and a final expert consensus, using the published criteria, determined whether each candidate represents a true T1-DRL.

T1-DRL criteria (Naval-Baudin et al., 2024):

  1. A hypointense rim continuous through at least two thirds of the edge of the white-matter part of the lesion.
  2. The rim corresponds to the edge, or at least part of the edge, of the T2-hyperintense lesion core.
  3. The lesion core is T2-hyperintense and does not enhance on post-contrast imaging.
  4. The rim (or part of it) is visible on at least two consecutive slices (for 2D imaging) or two orthogonal planes (for 3D imaging).

As a limitation, full application of these criteria would ideally include access to T2-FLAIR and multiplanar 3D reconstructions. Here we approximate the criteria based on the available imaging only, so please interpret the “gold standard” labels in that context.

Lesion segmentations are shown to help you locate the lesion of interest when other lesions appear in the cropped field of view. These masks are not intended as precise boundaries: their margins can be imperfect and should be treated purely as location guides, not exact segmentations.

Research participation

Help us understand how trainees recognise T1-DRLs. If you opt in, each scored batch is sent to a research server at api.navalbaudin.com when you click Submit batch. What's transmitted: an anonymous browser ID, the batch name, your per-lesion answers, plus any optional fields below. You can opt out at any time, and clearing local data will stop future submissions.

All fields are optional and only used to interpret submissions. To delete previously submitted data, contact the maintainer.
